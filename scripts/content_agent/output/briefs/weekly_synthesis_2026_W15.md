## 3. SURPRISING CONNECTIONS

**Kingsbury's "meat shield" + Arendt's *Eichmann in Jerusalem*** — This one lands. Arendt's insight was that the most dangerous compliance failures come not from malice but from people who follow procedure without moral judgment. Kingsbury is describing the same structure being engineered into AI governance: a human who bears legal accountability for outcomes they cannot meaningfully contest. The institutional form is identical; only the technology is new.

**Automated alignment researchers (Anthropic) + Arendt** — The same Arendt connection applies here with a different inflection: delegating oversight of alignment to the very system being aligned is a procedural solution to a moral problem. When the inspector and the inspected are the same entity, what Arendt called "thoughtlessness" becomes architecturally guaranteed.

**Bryan Cantrill on LLM laziness + Jane Jacobs' *Death and Life of Great American Cities*** — Genuinely surprising and correct. Jacobs' argument was that top-down planners, freed from the friction of living in the places they designed, produced inhuman scale without intelligence. Cantrill is describing the same dynamic: LLMs, freed from the cost of time and the discipline of constraint, produce accumulation without judgment. The structural isomorphism is real, not forced.
