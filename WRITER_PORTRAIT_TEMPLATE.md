# Writer Portrait
## A Living Document — Thomas Farnham

*This document is the context layer for the PKE Observer. It is not
a biography or a summary of facts. It is a map of patterns — how this
person thinks, writes, feels, and processes experience over time.*

*Construction process:*
*Phase 1 — The system generates a baseline portrait from corpus analysis*
*of journal entries and message threads. Specific observations, real*
*patterns, actual examples from the corpus. Not prompts — assertions.*
*Phase 2 — Thomas reacts. Confirms, corrects, adds what the corpus*
*couldn't see. The portrait becomes a dialogue.*
*Phase 3 — Every Observer interaction is a calibration opportunity.*
*The portrait gets sharper through use.*

*The system-generated baseline will be wrong in places. Those places*
*are where the most interesting refinement happens. A self-written*
*portrait is limited by self-knowledge and self-presentation. A*
*corpus-derived portrait sees patterns the writer can't see about*
*themselves.*

## Section 3 — Emotional Patterns

*How different emotional states show up in your prose. Not what you
feel — how the feeling changes the writing. The Observer needs to
recognise these patterns to respond appropriately.*

[ To be completed by Thomas ]

Prompts to consider:
- What does your writing look like when you're anxious?
  (sentence length, hedging language, returning to the same point,
  avoiding a conclusion?)
- What does it look like when you're clear and resolved?
- How do you write when you're processing something difficult in
  real time vs when you've already made peace with it?
- Is there a register you default to when you don't want to admit
  something to yourself?

## Section 4 — The Gap Between Feeling and Outcome

*Specific documented instances where what was feared didn't materialise.
Where the anxiety was disproportionate to what actually happened.
Where catastrophizing was a liability. This is the Temporal Mirror
material — the most powerful thing the Observer can surface.*

*This section is deliberately specific. Not "I tend to catastrophize"
but "in 2019 I was convinced X would happen and it didn't, and here
is what I wrote at the time."*

[ To be completed by Thomas — requires reviewing old journal entries ]

Format for each instance:
    Date of fear: approximate date of the worried entry
    What was feared: the specific anticipated outcome
    What actually happened: the resolution
    How long it took: the gap between fear and resolution
    What it cost: time, energy, decisions made from the fear
    Note reference: entry title or date if findable in corpus
